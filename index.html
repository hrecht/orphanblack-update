Orphan Black

1188 minutes of Tatiana Maslanys

Orphan Black, a scifi thriller from BBC America and Space, stars Canadian actor Tatiana Maslany as several clones, products of a secret military experiment. After the first season, a slew of reviews reported that she must be 99 percent of the show. I decided to test that claim. Prior to Orphan Black's third season, I analyzed lead actress Tatiana Maslany's time on screen in the first two seasons. The goal was simple: to determine just how much Tatiana Maslany was in each episode. This page, expanding upon that analysis, includes season three

Five of the clones appear regularly. Lead character Sarah, who returns home to reunite with her daughter, is the only clone to appear in all twenty. She is on screen nearly every minute of the first three episodes, often impersonating police officer Beth Childs, who makes an all-too-brief appearance in episode one. Sarah meets Canadian soccer mom Alison, American grad student Cosima, and Ukranian assassin Helena early in the first season. Fellow Brit "proclone" Rachel makes her entrance in episode nine. If that wasn't enough, five of her characters impersonate each other - for a total of eleven distinct "clone swaps."

See below for explanation of Jennifer's exclusion from this data.

Any one of these characters would be a major role for an actor. Sarah is on screen the most, appearing in every episode and about half of the show's time overall. Drawn separately, the main women's screen time in each episode seem like that of an average lead and several supporting actresses.

But Orphan Black is no average show. Add all of her characters and there is a Tatiana Maslany (or two - or three, or four) in nearly every minute of the series.

Three seasons of overlapping Tatiana Maslanys

So, just how much Tatiana Maslany is that? We could look at the empty spaces above, the few scenes in which she doesn't appear. But that wouldn't account for her many multi-clone scenes, in which several versions of herself appear at once. A better measure would be to look at her screen time, clone plus clone plus clone, in each episode.

Episode three is 42 minutes long. Her characters appear in that episode for a total of 54 minutes. There are more minutes of her characters than minutes of the show in ten episodes. After three seasons, there are 1244 minutes of Orphan Black and 1188 minutes of Tatiana Maslany.

About

The data for this page were collected using a strict, consistent, and highly enjoyable but not peer-reviewed method of rewatching all twenty episodes with a spreadsheet open and one hand on the pause button. Scene changes or other breaks less than 10 seconds were ignored. Recaps, title scenes, credits, and previews were excluded from analysis.

I collected the data for season one before season two's premiere. I decided to exclude home videos and corpses (RIP Beth and Katja) because including those scenes often repeated footage and also made it appear that Beth was alive and kicking in several episodes. For consistency, Jennifer's home videos in season two had to be treated equally and therefore her scenes are not included in this analysis.
